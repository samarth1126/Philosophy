Why to read philosophy?

Philosophy literally means "love for wisdom", from greek letters philo(love) and sophia(wisdom).

Philosophy predates modern science and mathematics as formal fields of study. It explores questions about reality (What exists?), knowledge (What can we know?), ethics (What is right?), and meaning (What is the purpose of life?).

These are major branches of Philosophy:

  • Metaphysics — nature of reality
  • Epistemology — nature of knowledge
  • Ethics — moral philosophy
  • Logic — rules of valid reasoning
  • Aesthetics — philosophy of art and beauty
